1. Feature Engineering (FE) & EDA Assignment

  • Due May 31 by 11:59pm
  • Points 20
  • Submitting a file upload

Important: This is a TWO part Assignment

Each Feature Engineering (FE) & EDA Assignment In Modules 1 to 4 will include two parts. Part 1 contains (a) your takeaways for the paper reading as well as (b) your data analysis on Jupyter or R Notebook. Part 2 will be a discussion that you will engage in with your classmates.

Note: The Video presentation and the report are due by 11:59 pm on the due date.

Part 1 Details

There are two tasks: (a) paper reading and (b) analysis. In (a), you will read the assigned article and record a short summary

 

Part 1. (a):

  • a one-minute presentation for the summary of your paper reading

Part 1. (b):

  • two-minute presentation/Executive Summary for your analysis. 
  • Note: Please make sure you dont reveal your code in your video presentation.

Part 2 Details

Below is the instruction for (b):

  • Select a free time-series dataset. If you do not know where to find a free time-series dataset, "Anomaly Detection for Time Series" has listed a few resources for you to start with.
  • Perform the following procedures in your work:
    • Simple moving average
    • Exponential smoothing
    • Seasonal-Trend Decomposition
    • Use the Prophet module
  • Conclusion: please justify each of your models why they can identify anomalies. 
  • Submit in the HTML format.  

Assessment

You will be assessed on (a) the quality of your takeaways, (b) the kind of features you develop and their rationale, the scope of your analysis (note: it must be holistic), and your written communication in the report. Please refer to the rubric for further details on the expectations.

Submission

To complete your submission,

  1. Click the blue Submit Assignment button at the top of this page.
  2. Click the Choose File button, and locate your submission.
    • (Preferred) Report and Code to be in one notebook (Jupyter or R Notebook from RStudio)
      • Submit the report as HTML and the code as the notebook source.
    • Report and code can also be separate:
      • The code in one R file.
      • The Report as an HTML file.
  1. Feel free to include a comment with your submission.
  2. Finally, click the blue Submit Assignment button.
